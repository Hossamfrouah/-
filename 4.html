The Grand Egyptian Museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum The idea of establishing the Grand Egyptian Museum began in the nineties of the last century, and in 2002 the foundation stone for the museum project was laid to be built in a privileged location overlooking the eternal Giza pyramids, where the Egyptian state is under the auspices of the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO). UNESCO) and the International Federation of Architects, for an international architectural competition for the best museum design, and the current design submitted by Heneghan Peng Architects of Ireland won, and its design was based on the representation of sunlight extending from the tops of the three pyramids when it met a conical mass that is

the Grand Egyptian Museum. The museum construction project began in May 2005, when the site was prepared and equipped, and in 2006 the largest antiquities restoration center in the Middle East was established, which is dedicated to the restoration, preservation, maintenance and rehabilitation of antiquities. For display in the halls of the museum, which opened in 2010. The Prime Minister’s Decree No. 2795 of 2016 was issued to establish and organize the Egyptian Authority for Major Museums, prior to the issuance of Law No. 9 of 2020 reorganizing the Museums Authority as a general economic authority reporting to the Minister concerned with antiquities affairs. His Excellency the President of the Republic issued a decision to form the Board of Trustees of the Grand Egyptian Museums Authority headed by His Excellency and the membership of a number of distinguished personalities and Egyptian and international experts. While the museum is managed by a board of directors headed by the minister and with membership: CEO of the Major Egyptian Museums Authority, Secretary General of the Supreme Council of Antiquities, CEO of the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization, and CEO of the Egyptian Museums Authority. The General Authority for Tourism Promotion, the CEO of the General Authority for Investment and Free Zones, the Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Egyptian Federation of Tourist Chambers, the legal advisor to the Minister, and a number of experts specialized in the fields of (antiquities, economics, law, management, international cooperation and marketing). The construction of the museum building, which covers an area of more than 300,000 square meters, was completed in 2021,

to include a number of exhibition halls, one of which is larger than many museums in Egypt and the world. The museum is considered one of the most important and greatest achievements of modern Egypt. It was established to be an integrated global cultural and entertainment edifice, and to be the first destination for everyone interested in the ancient Egyptian heritage, as the largest museum in the world that tells the story of the history of ancient Egyptian civilization, as it contains a large number of distinctive and unique artifacts, including the treasures of the golden king Tutankhamun. Amun, which is on display for the first time in its entirety since the discovery of his tomb in November 1922, as well as the collection of Queen Hetepe's personal guard. The mother of King Khufu, the builder of the Great Pyramid in Giza, as well as the King Khufu Boat Museum, in addition to many antiquities from the pre-dynastic era to the Greek and Roman eras.

The museum includes places for cultural activities and events such as a children’s museum, an educational center, temporary exhibition halls, a cinema, and a conference center, in addition to many commercial areas that include shops, cafeterias and restaurants, in addition to parks and gardens. gardens. . In April 2021, a contract was signed to provide and operate visitor services at the museum with the Hassan Allam Al-Fayez Alliance, which includes Egyptian and international companies with diverse experiences in the fields of business administration, marketing, hospitality, promotion, quality, and occupational health and safety. To achieve its objectives, the museum specializes in the following:

Displaying archaeological collections and using the latest museum display methods and techniques. Digital documentation, registration, preservation, insurance, study, maintenance and restoration of artifacts. Organizing temporary and permanent antiquities exhibitions inside Egypt. Holding seminars, conferences, cultural and scientific activities and other activities. Educating the Egyptian youth and society about the Egyptian civilization.
